Android

Android is a mobile operating system developed by Google, based on the Linux kernel. It is designed primarily for touchscreen devices such as smartphones and tablets. Android provides a rich application framework that allows developers to create innovative apps and games. The operating system is known for its flexibility and customization options, making it popular among manufacturers and users alike. iOS

iOS is Apple's proprietary operating system designed for iPhones, iPads, and iPod Touch devices. Introduced in 2007, it provides a smooth and intuitive user experience, featuring a streamlined interface, a strong focus on security, and a wide range of apps available through the App Store. iOS integrates tightly with Apple's ecosystem, allowing seamless interaction with other Apple devices and services. It supports regular updates and offers robust developer tools for creating apps optimized for Apple's hardware.

HarmonyOS

HarmonyOS is an operating system developed by Huawei. Launched in 2019, it's designed to work across a wide range of devices, including smartphones,..etc. HarmonyOS aims to create a seamless and unified user experience across different types of devices by leveraging a microkernel architecture. This architecture allows for greater flexibility and security, with the OS able to scale and adapt to various hardware and screen sizes. HarmonyOS also supports a multi-device interaction model, where devices can work together more harmoniously.
